Phase 2: Beyond the Vicious Circle

Education, Income, and Demographics across 278 Israeli Settlements

Data: Bituach Leumi (Dec 2024) + CBS + LAMAS

Phase 1 established the correlation between socio-economic vulnerability and disability (Spearman r = 0.58). In Phase 2 we ask: what drives the vicious circle? We add education levels, average salaries, and demographic composition (Arab %, Haredi %) to understand which factors are independent predictors and which are proxies for the same underlying signal.

Education & Disability

% with Academic Degree vs General Disability Rate | Spearman r = -0.59

Key Finding

Higher education correlates with lower disability rates (r = -0.59). But is education a cause of better health, or a consequence of better socio-economic conditions? Settlements with more graduates tend to be wealthier — so education may be a proxy for the same structural advantage captured by the SE index.

Salary & Disability

Average Monthly Salary vs General Disability Rate | Spearman r = -0.60

Key Finding

Salary is almost perfectly correlated with the SE index (r = 0.94) — it is essentially the same signal. This confirms that salary does not add independent explanatory power: it is a component of socio-economic status, not a separate driver.

The Demographic Divide

Arab Population % vs General Disability Rate | Spearman r = 0.58

Key Finding

Arab-majority settlements show higher disability rates — but is this ethnicity or poverty? Arab settlements are disproportionately represented in low SE clusters. The correlation (r = 0.58) may largely reflect the overlap between Arab population concentration and structural disadvantage.

The Haredi Puzzle

Haredi Population % vs General Disability Rate | Spearman r = 0.22

Key Finding

Haredi communities have low socio-economic scores but only slightly elevated disability rates. They break the vicious circle pattern. The weak correlation (r = 0.22) suggests that community-level factors — social cohesion, family support networks, or different patterns of benefit claiming — may buffer against the poverty-disability link observed in other populations.
